Bio

Currently serving as an Analyst in the Information Management Section, Kinmen County Government, I hold a B.S. in Computer Science and an M.S. in Multimedia Engineering from National Chiao Tung University (2008–2014, now National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University) and was inducted into the Phi Tau Phi Scholastic Honor Society in 2015.

In 2015, one year after completing my graduate studies, I passed Taiwan’s Local Government Civil Service Special Examination (Information Processing) and began serving in the Penghu County Government.

In 2017, I was admitted to Ph.D. programs in Electrical Engineering at both National Tsing Hua University and National Chiao Tung University. However, in 2020, after careful reflection, I chose to withdraw, recognizing that my enduring intellectual interests lay beyond pure coding and technical specialization.

In 2022, after passing Taiwan’s Senior Civil Service Examination (Information Processing), I continued my public service career at the Kinmen County Government. In 2024, I again passed the Senior Civil Service Examination in the same field but declined reassignment, choosing to remain in Kinmen.

While remaining committed to public service, I have continued to pursue broader intellectual development. I aim to integrate my background in computer engineering with legal inquiry. I am currently transitioning toward legal studies, with the long-term goal of engaging in technology law.

This space（GitHub） documents my ongoing studies, reflections, and explorations at the intersection of engineering, governance, and law.
